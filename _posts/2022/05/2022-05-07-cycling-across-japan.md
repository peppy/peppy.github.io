Cycling is one of my life-long hobbies, and also my main means of staying in shape. I'm writing this post mostly for my own records but sharing it in a hope that it may be helpful or at very least interesting to some of you.

---

[ハチミツとクローバ (Honey and Clover)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Honey_and_Clover) is one of my favourite anime series to this day. While watching it (over two decades ago!)  I was quite emotionally attached to the characters and story progression. One specific arc in the story sees a character who has lost his direction in life decide impulsively to cycle as far as he can without looking back.

He ends up cycling a good portion of Japan, and learning much about himself.

I've ever since had a lifelong goal of cycling the whole of Japan. Of course, Japan is a big country – cycling the full length would be over 2,000km!!. I'm no professionally trained cyclist – sure I've done a few 60-80km rides in the past, but doing more than that distance in a day would definitely be pushing it, so that's quite some distance to travel.

With my busy life of maintaining a game played by millions of players while ALSO trying to rewrite that game on the side, such a goal seems unattainable.

Of course you could argue that I could just drop everything for a few months and make it happen. But I suffer from very high self expectations and drive to keep osu! going strong, so that's just not going to happen. But when I was talking to a friend about their upcoming trip to Japan and they mentioned planning a cycling journey across a smaller portion of the country, I jumped on the opportunity to ask whether I could tag along – and luckily they accepted!

So the cogs started turning to plan cycling trip which ended up spanning just shy of 1,000km, but had time allowances in it for both my friend's plans to meet his various Splatoon acquaintances across the country, and my own dev time requirements on a daily basis.

Society, technology and everything around us is changing faster than ever. Most of the social discourse and news that is propagated is negative, uncomfortable and sometimes just outright scary. You may call it coping, but I make the personal choice not to follow world events and stay as uninformed as possible. It keeps me sane.

Call it luck or call it genes, I've never felt anything like burnout, stress or depression.

With that said, during covid due to lack of social interactions I optimised my life to allow me to be the most efficient dev I could be. My productivity skyrocketed, but in that time my fitness dropped significantly, and while I was still very happy, life became a touch mundane.

This cycling journey reinforced that witnessing and interacting with people in smaller towns and of different cultures, living their lives with completely different perspectives and outlooks on life to your own is eye-opening for sure.

Interacting with people and experiencing what nature has to offer is invigorating. Pushing yourself beyond the limits you thought your body had is painful but rewarding.

If you're not in the best place mentally, maybe it's time to do something crazy like cycling in one direction as far as you can. It might help set you straight just a bit.
